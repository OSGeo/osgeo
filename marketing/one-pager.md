# OSGeo: Open Source Geospatial Foundation  
### One-Page Marketing Overview


## What is OSGeo?
The Open Source Geospatial Foundation (OSGeo) is a global, non-profit organization dedicated to supporting and promoting open-source geospatial software, standards, and education. We empower individuals, governments, and organizations to build better solutions with free and open tools.



## Our Mission:
To foster global innovation by supporting collaborative development of open-source geospatial technologies and accessible geospatial education for all.



## What OSGeo Provides?

### **✔ Open Source Software Ecosystem**
OSGeo hosts and supports world-class geospatial projects, including:

- **QGIS** – Desktop GIS  
- **GRASS GIS** – Advanced geospatial analysis  
- **GDAL/OGR** – Geospatial data translation library  
- **GeoServer & MapServer** – Web mapping servers  
- **PostGIS** – Spatial database extension for PostgreSQL  
- **PROJ** – Coordinate transformation library  
- **PDAL** – Point cloud processing  

These tools are free, open, and used worldwide in research, industry, and government.



### **✔ Community & Collaboration**
- Global developer community  
- Local chapters around the world  
- Working groups for documentation, marketing, systems, incubation, and education  



### **✔ Standards & Interoperability**
OSGeo works with OGC and other organizations to ensure open standards and interoperability between tools.



### **✔ Education & Training**
- Open geospatial course material  
- Workshops and tutorials  
- University outreach  
- Mentorship programs (e.g., Google Summer of Code)



### **✔ Events: FOSS4G**
Our flagship event **FOSS4G** is the world’s largest open-source geospatial conference, attracting developers, researchers, students, and industry leaders from around the globe.
